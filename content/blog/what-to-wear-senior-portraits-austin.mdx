Your outfit won't make or break your senior portraits — but it comes close.

The right clothing choice elevates everything: it helps the light fall better, keeps attention on *you* instead of what you're wearing, and makes your images feel timeless rather than dated. After photographing hundreds of Austin seniors, I've seen what works and what doesn't.

Here's the practical, honest guide — so you can stop stressing about this part and show up ready to have fun.

## Start With Colors That Love You Back

This is the single most important thing: **skip the trendy neons and the pure whites.** The colors that photograph beautifully for Austin senior portraits are:

- **Neutrals and earth tones** — cream, camel, sage, rust, dusty rose, warm gray
- **Deep jewel tones** — navy, burgundy, forest green, deep plum
- **Soft pastels** — blush, lavender, sky blue (when used thoughtfully)
- **Classic black** — always elegant, always flattering, always works

Pure white can blow out in outdoor Austin light and create unflattering contrast. Neons and very bright colors can cast color onto your skin in harsh sunlight. Stick to colors you'd actually wear, in versions that are a little more muted.

**A practical tip:** Hold your outfit up to your face in natural light before the session. Does it make your skin glow, or does it fight with it? That's your answer.

## How Many Outfits Should You Bring?

For a standard senior portrait session in Austin, **2-3 outfits** is the sweet spot. This gives you:

- Variety without chaos
- A mix of casual, dressy, and something personal (a jersey, a band tee, something that feels like *you*)
- Enough looks to tell a complete senior year story

One outfit should feel like your "main look" — the one that goes on the walls and in the announcements. The others are personality shots, candids, and the images you send your friends.

**For guys:** A well-fitted blazer or sport coat over a simple shirt is hard to beat. Bring a second casual option and you're set.

**For girls:** A dress or skirt-and-top combo for one look, something more casual (jeans and a great top) for another, and a third that feels personal to you — a team uniform, your school colors, anything that represents your senior year.

## What to Avoid

After years of shooting [senior portraits in Austin](/senior-portrait-photographer-austin), here's what consistently causes regret:

**Logos and busy graphics.** Graphic tees with large logos or busy prints draw the eye away from your face and can look distracting — especially in close-up portraits. Subtle logos are fine; giant ones aren't.

**Clothes that don't fit well.** Anything too tight, too loose, or uncomfortable will show. You'll spend the session adjusting instead of relaxing. Wear clothes that fit your body *right now*, not the body you think you should have by then.

**Stiff, formal clothes you've never worn.** If it doesn't feel like you, it won't look like you. Authenticity always photographs better than "dressed up."

**Matching exactly with others.** If you're doing group shots with friends, coordinate — don't match. Coordinated color palettes look intentional. Identical outfits look like a sports team photo.

## Accessories and Details

The right accessories pull a look together without overwhelming it:

- **Jewelry:** Keep it simple. One statement piece — a necklace or earrings, not both — is usually the right call. Dainty everyday jewelry always works.
- **Shoes:** They show up more than you think, especially in full-body shots. Clean, well-maintained shoes that complement your outfit matter.
- **Bags and props:** If there's something meaningful — a musical instrument, a sports item, a book — bring it. These details make your portraits uniquely yours.
- **Hair:** Do your hair as you normally would, maybe one step up. Overly structured updos can look stiff; loose, natural styles tend to photograph beautifully.

## Austin-Specific Tips

Shooting senior photos in Austin means dealing with a few specifics that matter:

**Plan for the heat.** Even in spring and fall, it gets warm. Avoid thick fabrics that will make you sweat visibly. Breathable fabrics (linen, cotton blends, lightweight knits) are your friends.

**Outdoor locations in Austin can be dusty.** If we're shooting at Barton Creek Greenbelt, Zilker Park, or other natural settings, consider the terrain. Flowy maxi dresses are gorgeous but can drag. Heels can sink in soft ground.

**Golden hour is real.** Austin's golden hour light — about an hour before sunset — is absolutely stunning and the most flattering time to shoot. If your session is scheduled for late afternoon, your photos will thank you. Dress in warmer tones to complement that light.

**Have a backup plan.** If your perfect outfit is weather-dependent (white sundress, winter coat), have an alternative ready. Austin weather keeps us guessing.
