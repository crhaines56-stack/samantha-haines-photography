## The Right Time to Book — and Why It Matters

If you're researching maternity photographers in Austin, you've probably already heard the 28–34 week window. That's real guidance, not just marketing copy.

At 28 weeks, your bump is unmistakably present. By 34 weeks, it's full and round — visually powerful — while most women are still physically comfortable enough to move, shift, and actually enjoy themselves during the session. Past 36 weeks, fatigue and discomfort tend to take over, and it shows.

Book your session date early. Not because spots are artificially scarce, but because good timing requires flexibility. If something shifts — your due date gets adjusted, you have an off week — you want room to reschedule without scrambling.

## What to Wear (and What Not to Stress About)

For [Austin maternity sessions](/maternity-photographer-austin), less is often more — in terms of busy patterns, loud colors, and overly constructed clothing. Simple shapes photograph better. Movement photographs better.

Flowing gowns. Draped fabrics. A fitted dress that doesn't fight the shape of your body. These work.

Vue Studio also has an in-house wardrobe. If you're not sure what to bring, or you've been staring at your closet for three weeks without landing on anything, don't worry. Come in with an idea or two and let Samantha's team help you fill in the gaps.

One thing worth saying plainly: the goal is never to make you look like someone else. It's to make you look like you — at this exact moment, in the best possible light.
